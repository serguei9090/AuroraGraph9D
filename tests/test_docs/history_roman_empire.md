# The Fall of the Roman Empire: Key Events and Dates

## Overview
The Western Roman Empire officially fell on September 4, 476 AD when Germanic chieftain Odoacer deposed the last Roman emperor, Romulus Augustulus. This event is widely considered one of the most significant turning points in world history.

## Key Timeline

### The Crisis of the Third Century (235-284 AD)
Between 235 AD and 284 AD, the Roman Empire nearly collapsed under the combined pressures of invasion, civil war, plague, and economic depression. During this 49-year period, at least 26 claimants to the title of Emperor rose and fell. The Plague of Cyprian, which began in 249 AD, killed an estimated 5,000 people per day in Rome at its peak.

### Diocletian's Reforms (284-305 AD)
Emperor Diocletian introduced the Tetrarchy system in 293 AD, dividing the empire into four administrative regions, each governed by a separate ruler. He also enacted the Edict on Maximum Prices in 301 AD, which set fixed prices for over 1,200 goods and services to combat inflation. The penalty for exceeding these prices was death.

### The Sack of Rome (410 AD)
On August 24, 410 AD, the Visigoth king Alaric I led his forces into Rome. This was the first time in 800 years that Rome had been captured by a foreign enemy. The sacking lasted exactly three days, ending on August 27, 410 AD. Alaric demanded 5,000 pounds of gold, 30,000 pounds of silver, 4,000 silken tunics, 3,000 hides dyed scarlet, and 3,000 pounds of pepper as ransom.

### The Battle of the Catalaunian Plains (451 AD)
On June 20, 451 AD, a coalition of Roman and Visigothic forces under General Flavius Aetius defeated Attila the Hun near Chalons in modern-day France. The battle resulted in an estimated 162,000 to 300,000 casualties on both sides. Attila retreated and never invaded Gaul again.

### The Final Fall (476 AD)
Romulus Augustulus, who was only 16 years old, was deposed by Odoacer on September 4, 476 AD. Unlike previous depositions, Odoacer did not name a new emperor. Instead, he sent the imperial regalia to Constantinople, effectively ending the Western Roman Empire after 503 years of rule.

## Economic Data
At its peak under Emperor Trajan (98-117 AD), the Roman Empire had an estimated GDP equivalent of $43.4 billion in modern terms. The empire's population at that time was approximately 70 million people, with the city of Rome itself housing roughly 1.2 million inhabitants.

## The Eastern Survival
The Eastern Roman Empire (Byzantine Empire) survived the fall of the west and continued for another 977 years until the Fall of Constantinople on May 29, 1453, when Ottoman Sultan Mehmed II conquered the city after a 53-day siege using cannons that fired 600-pound stone balls.
